ALAA MASO

Athlete-Info:



First Olympic Games: Tokyo 2020


Year of Birth: 2000


Games Participations: 1


Country: IOC Refugee Olympic Team


Athlete: Swimming





BIOGRAPHY:


Alaa used to live in Aleppo with his family, and began swimming at an early age thanks to his father, who became a swimming coach following his retirement from the army. He trained as a swimmer and a triathlete and knew fellow refugee swimmer Yusra Mardini when they were both young.



Alaa left Syria in 2015 after his training facilities were damaged and he felt the pressure of the conflict all around him. Following a long journey across Europe, he settled in Germany and began swimming training once again with Emil Guliyev.


He has also returned to school and is making up on the years lost due to leaving Syria.


Alaa competed in Tokyo 2020 in the 50m freestyle. He is now aiming for Paris 2024 and hopes to compete in two events: 50m and 100m freestyle.


After competing at the Olympic Games, Alaa had the honour of signing the “Golden Book” of Hannover at the Town Hall, marking the history of the city as the first refugee athlete to qualify for such a major sporting event.
