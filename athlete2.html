RAYSSA LEAL

Athlete-Info:



First Olympic Games: Tokyo 2020


Year of Birth: 2008


Olympic Medal: 1 Silver Medal


Games Participations: 1


Country: Team Brazil


Athlete: SkateBoarding





BIOGRAPHY:


Born in 2008, the 15-year-old Leal rose to prominence in a way only the youngest generation really know how – through the medium of viral video. A short video clip of Leal perfectly executing a heelflip down a set of stairs while donning a bright blue tutu as part of a fairy princess costume perfect exploded across social media platforms, propelling the then-7-year-old to internet notoriety.


At 11 the goofy-footed skateboarder became the youngest street skater ever to win a Women’s Final on the Street League Skateboarding World Tour in Los Angeles. She bested current world number one Pamela Rosa to clinch the title. The Brazilian – who has been skating for eight years - featured twice at the X-Games in 2019, finishing fourth in both competitions. She clinched her first X-Games win in 2022, taking the street event at X-Games Chiba.


Just ahead of the Olympic Games Tokyo 2020 in 2021, Leal finished third in the Street World Championships in Rome. She finished once again ahead of Rosa and their fellow countrywoman Leticia Bufoni to take a podium spot.


The eyes of the world haven’t stopped watching Leal skate since she first appeared on their smart screens. And at Tokyo 2020, they saw her clinch street silver behind Momiji Nishiya of Japan.


Now, she's back in action at the Street World Championships Sharjah 2022, held in January and February 2023 and serving as an indirect Olympic qualifier where Leal can earn ranking points. If she makes it to Paris as many expect her to, Leal should be able to wow the crowds in person at the Games.
